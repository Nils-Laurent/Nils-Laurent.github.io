Research

Time-frequency analysis of non-stationnary multicomponent signals is a very active field of research. My contribution is strongly related to the Fourier based synchrosqueezing transform.

In the context of my thesis, I worked on instantaneous frequency estimation and on the reconstruction of a signal affected by white gaussian noise. The resulting techniques are based on the local analysis of the synchrosqueezing operators and their robustness depending on the level of noise. My thesis also includes the applicative study of the non-invasive estimation of the fetal heart rate from two ECG channels. For this specific task, I invesigated how to take advantage of the harmonic structure of the signal and proceed to source separation using the non-negative matrix factorization.
